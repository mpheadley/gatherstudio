# Domain Name Strategy Guide

> Reference for choosing a domain name for client projects.
> Walk through this with the client before purchasing.

---

## The Three Approaches

| Approach | Example | Pros | Cons |
|----------|---------|------|------|
| **Brand** | `dartsiderentals.com` | Clean, memorable, professional | No SEO keyword boost from domain |
| **SEO/Local** | `rockmartnerfparties.com` | Targets local + service keywords | Longer, less brandable, harder to say aloud |
| **Hybrid** | `dartsideparties.com` or `dartsiderockmart.com` | Balances brand + a keyword or location | Slightly longer than pure brand |

---

## Decision Framework

1. **Is the business name short and clear?** → Go brand-first (`joesplumbing.com`)
2. **Is the business name long or generic?** → Go hybrid with a keyword (`joesplumbinganniston.com`)
3. **Does the business serve one specific city?** → Consider adding the city (`rockmart`, `anniston`)
4. **Will the business expand beyond one city?** → Keep the domain broad, use page titles and content for geo keywords

---

## What Actually Matters More Than the Domain

The domain name provides a minor SEO signal at best. These carry far more weight:

- **Page `<title>` tag** — this is what Google actually displays and weighs heavily. A good title like "Nerf Party Rentals in Rockmart, GA | Dart Side Rentals" does the SEO work regardless of the domain.
- **`<meta name="description">`** — write a compelling 150-character description with service + city + CTA
- **Google Business Profile** — the #1 way local businesses get found. Link the website, set correct categories and services.
- **Content on the page** — headings, body copy, and JSON-LD schema carry the real keyword signals

---

## Practical Steps

1. **Check availability:** Use [Namecheap](https://www.namecheap.com) or [Google Domains](https://domains.google) to search
2. **Grab the brand domain** even if you go hybrid — it's cheap insurance ($10-15/year)
3. **Prefer `.com`** — `.net` and `.co` work but `.com` has the most trust for local businesses
4. **Avoid hyphens and numbers** — hard to say aloud, easy to mistype
5. **Say it out loud 3 times** — if you stumble, it's too long or confusing
6. **Check for conflicts** — search the name on Google, Facebook, and your state's business registry

---

## Consultant Tip

> If the brand domain is available, grab it. But make sure the **Page Title** in your code is what does the heavy lifting for local keywords. The domain is the sign on the building — the title tag is the ad in the phone book.
